# **CyberSafe LA: Cybersecurity Clinic Proposal**

## **Overview**
CyberSafe LA is a proposed student‑run cybersecurity consulting clinic designed to support small businesses across Los Angeles. The concept was developed as part of a graduate cybersecurity project at **California State University, Los Angeles (Cal State LA)** in response to the growing cybersecurity gap affecting local small and medium‑sized businesses (SMBs).

Small businesses represent **more than 99% of all businesses in Los Angeles County**, yet most operate without dedicated IT staff or cybersecurity resources. This leaves thousands of organizations (including restaurants, retail shops, medical offices, nonprofits, and professional service firms) vulnerable to increasingly sophisticated cyber threats.

The **2025 Verizon Data Breach Investigations Report (DBIR)** highlights the severity of this issue:
- **88%** of SMB malware-related breaches involve ransomware  
- **33%** of SMB breaches stem from stolen credentials  
- Third‑party involvement in breaches has **doubled** year‑over‑year to **30%**

CyberSafe LA aims to directly address these risks by providing accessible, high‑quality cybersecurity guidance to underserved communities.

---

## **Mission**
To empower small businesses in Los Angeles with practical, affordable, and impactful cybersecurity support delivered by trained graduate students under faculty supervision, while simultaneously creating hands‑on learning opportunities for cybersecurity students.

---

## **Program Structure**
CyberSafe LA is modeled after Cal State LA’s successful **Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA)** program and informed by the **NSA/CISA Identity and Access Management (IAM) Best Practices** framework.

Graduate students in the MSIS Cybersecurity program serve as consultants across three service tiers:

### **Tier 1: Foundational Services**
These entry-level services target the most common SMB vulnerabilities:
- Password Hygiene Workshop 
- Phishing Awareness Training 
- Wi-Fi Security Assessment 
- Basic Website Vulnerability Scan 
- Device & Software Inventory 
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) Clinic
  - Directly addresses the DBIR finding that credential abuse is the leading SMB breach vector.

### **Tier 2: Intermediate Services**
- Policy development  
- Basic compliance guidance  
- Secure configuration support  
- Risk assessment walkthroughs  

### **Tier 3: Advanced Services**
- Supervised penetration testing  
- Incident response planning  
- Vendor and third‑party risk evaluation  

---

## **Funding & Sustainability**
CyberSafe LA is designed with a long-term sustainability model supported by:
- **University budget allocation**
- **Federal grants**  
  - CISA CETAP  
  - NSF Scholarship for Service (SFS)
- **Google Cybersecurity Clinics Fund**  
  - A national grant program supporting university-based cybersecurity clinics  
- **Partnerships with:** 
  - LA Regional Small Business Development Center (SBDC)  
  - East LA Chamber of Commerce  
  - CISA Region 9  

These partnerships strengthen community reach and ensure the clinic can operate at scale.

---

## **Impact**
CyberSafe LA has the potential to:
- Reduce cybersecurity risk for thousands of small businesses  
- Provide workforce development opportunities for cybersecurity students  
- Strengthen regional cyber resilience  
- Expand access to cybersecurity resources in underserved communities  
- Serve as a replicable model for other universities
